The Goal of the Game

Are you a soldier... or a saboteur? The players are soldiers working their trenches ever-deeper into the enemy terretory in search of fame, or saboteurs trying to put obstacles in the soldiers’ path. The members of each group should work together, but often they can only guess who their allies are! If the soldiers can make a path to the objective, they are rewarded with ranks while the saboteurs leave empty-handed. If the soldiers fail, it is the saboteurs who reap the reward! But, the identity of each soldier is not revealed until the ranks is split up. After three rounds, the soldier with the highest rank wins!

Preparation

The number of soldier and saboteur cards used depends on the number of players:

  • 3 players: 1 saboteur and 3 soldiers
  • 4 players: 1 saboteur and 4 soldiers
  • 5 players: 2 saboteurs and 4 soldiers
  • 6 players: 2 saboteurs and 5 soldiers
  • 7 players: 3 saboteurs and 5 soldiers
  • 8 players: 3 saboteurs and 6 soldiers
  • 9 players: 3 saboteurs and 7 soldiers
  • 10 players: all soldiers cards

How to play

On his turn, a player must first play 1 card.

This means either:

    - Add 1 path card to the maze; or - Place 1 action card in front of any player; or - Pass, by putting 1 card face down on the discard pile.
Then, the player must draw the top card off the path deck and add it to his hand. This ends his turn, and play passes to the next player. Note: When the draw deck is used up, the players do not draw cards anymore—a turn then consists only of playing a card.
